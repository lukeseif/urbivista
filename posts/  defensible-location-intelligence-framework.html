SITE SELECTION

How to Evaluate Sites with Defensible Location Intelligence

Published Jan 12, 2026 · 8–10 minute read

What “defensible” means

Defensible location intelligence is not “more data.” It’s a process that makes your decision explainable: what you prioritized, what you assumed, how sensitive the outcome is, and what would change your recommendation.

The 5-step evaluation framework

  1. Define the decision: success metrics, timeline, and constraints.
  2. Build comparable site scoring: access, demand, competition, costs, and operational feasibility.
  3. Document assumptions: sources, confidence, and what would invalidate them.
  4. Run sensitivity tests: best/base/worst cases and key drivers.
  5. Produce an executive-ready narrative: recommendation + risks + mitigations.

What to include in your site selection scorecard

  • Demand: target customer presence, trip drivers, growth, seasonality.
  • Accessibility: turning movements, ingress/egress, visibility, adjacency.
  • Competition: direct alternatives, substitution risk, response likelihood.
  • Unit economics: rent, TI, capex, taxes, labor, delivery radius (if relevant).
  • Execution risk: permitting, utilities, construction schedule, entitlements.

Pro tip: store assumptions like data

If you can’t point to the assumption behind a KPI, you can’t defend the KPI.
