How to Evaluate Sites with Defensible Location Intelligence

Most site selection processes fail at the same point: assumptions aren’t documented, tradeoffs aren’t clear, and approvals stall. Here’s a practical framework to fix that.

1) Start with decision criteria

Define what “good” means before you look at properties: demand, access, workforce, competition, cost, and risk.

2) Use consistent trade areas

Apples-to-apples comparisons require consistent drive-times, rings, or customer catchments.

3) Score, compare, document

Make the decision defensible by recording sources, assumptions, and why one site wins.
